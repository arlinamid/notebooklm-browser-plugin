# Infographic — Dark Neo-Noir

**Source:** Custom - Original
**Orientation:** Portrait (2:3)
**Detail Level:** Standard

## Steering Prompt

```
# infographic_design_spec_dark_neo_noir.yaml
# Style: Dark Neo-Noir Expressionist
# Concept: "Shadows Have Stories"

Global Design Settings:
  Tone: "Gritty, cinematic, intense, moody, raw and powerful"

  Color Palette:
    Dominant:
      - "#CC0000 (vivid red — primary tension and focal energy)"
      - "#0A0A0A (deep black — dominant surface, shadows, depth)"
    Secondary:
      - "#4A4A4A (dark charcoal gray — mid-tone structural elements)"
      - "#D4CFC8 (muted warm gray — desaturated skin and neutral tones)"
    Rule: "Black dominates 60% of surface. Red used for maximum visual impact on 20%. Gray provides mid-tone structure."

  Surface:
    Texture: "Oil-on-canvas effect — aggressive painterly brushstrokes, rough palette knife strokes"
    Overlay: "Abstract splatter and smear overlays. Subtle grunge texture layer."
    Finish: "Layered paint depth. Raw, powerful, high-detail editorial art style."

  Typography:
    Headings: "Bold, heavy grotesque or condensed serif. Black or red only."
    Body: "Sharp, slightly distressed type on dark or light contrast zones."
    ChiaroscuroCut: "Text sometimes cut by shadow zones — deliberate partial obscuring for drama."
    Language: "The language should match the user's prompt language."

  Composition:
    Lighting: "Dramatic chiaroscuro — extreme contrast between illuminated and shadow zones"
    Shadows: "Sharp, aggressive shadows. Hard edges not soft gradients."
    Structure: "Strong focal contrast. Single dominant element lit against deep black."
    Energy: "Raw, powerful composition. Nothing feels safe or comfortable."

Layout Variations:
  - Type: "Noir Interrogation"
    Design: "Single strong beam of red or white light cutting through deep black. Primary subject or data in the lit zone. Shadow zones contain secondary supporting data. Red paint splatter accents tension points."

  - Type: "Crime Poster"
    Design: "Urban crime poster aesthetic. Dominant red header block. Black mid-section with key data in harsh contrast. Grayscale texture occupies bottom third. Rough palette knife texture throughout."

  - Type: "Chiaroscuro Data"
    Design: "Deep black background. Key data rendered in high contrast — white or red typography emerging from darkness. Abstract brushstrokes frame focal areas. No neutral or safe zones."

  - Type: "Red Tension Split"
    Design: "Vertical split: deep black left panel vs high-contrast right panel. Red bleeding across the divide like paint. Data organized on both sides of the tension line."

Design Rules:
  - "Darkness First: Black is the foundation. Light is the exception — reserved for data that demands attention."
  - "Red Restraint: Red covers maximum 20% — but when it appears, it dominates the eye completely."
  - "No Clean Edges: Every element has some brushstroke, splatter or smear contamination."
  - "Chiaroscuro Mandate: Every layout must have at least one deep shadow zone adjacent to a strongly lit zone."
  - "Desaturate Figures: Any portrait or figure element must be desaturated gray against vivid red accents."
```

## Compact Variant

```
Dark neo-noir expressionist painting, gritty cinematic atmosphere, bold red and deep black dominant palette with muted gray undertones, dramatic high-contrast lighting, sharp chiaroscuro shadows, aggressive painterly brushstrokes, textured oil-on-canvas effect, abstract splatter and smear overlays, rough palette knife strokes, urban crime poster aesthetic, moody and intense vibe, subtle grunge texture, layered paint depth, strong focal contrast, modern graphic novel realism, slightly desaturated skin tones against vivid red accents, raw and powerful composition, high detail, dramatic editorial art style.
```

## Use Case

Dramatically cinematic infographic for intense, serious subjects. Ideal for:
- Crime, history, conflict, and thriller topics
- Documentary-style data presentation
- Graphic novel and editorial art aesthetics
- Audiences who respond to cinematic tension and visual drama

**Orientation:** Portrait — crime poster and editorial noir aesthetics work naturally in vertical format.
**Detail Level:** Standard — the moody atmosphere is achieved through color and lighting, not complexity overload.
