# Royal Blue & Red Watercolor

**Source:** kottley
**Format:** Slide Deck
**Difficulty:** Beginner

## Prompt

```
# presentation_design_spec_watercolor_blue_red.yaml
# Style: Royal Blue & Red Watercolor
# Concept: "Painterly Depth — Artistic Fluidity with Structured Clarity"

Global Design Settings:
  Tone: "Artistic, refined, painterly, creative-academic, emotionally resonant"

  Color Palette:
    Base: "#F8F6F2 (warm off-white — watercolor paper texture)"
    Primary:
      - "#1A3A8F (royal blue — deep, saturated)"
      - "#CC2200 (crimson red — bold, warm)"
    Secondary:
      - "#4A6CC4 (medium blue — mid-tone washes)"
      - "#E64A19 (burnt orange-red — transition tone)"
    Neutrals:
      - "#2D2D2D (charcoal — for text)"
      - "#FFFFFF (white — negative space and highlights)"
    Technique: "All colored elements rendered as wet watercolor washes — translucent, layered, with soft wet-to-wet bleed edges."

  Typography:
    Headings: "Elegant serif or calligraphic type. Feels like written with a fine brush or dip pen."
    Body: "Clean humanist serif or refined sans serif. Small, high readability against light backgrounds."
    Accent: "Handwritten annotations or short captions in a brushstroke-style script."
    Language: "The language should be what users said in the prompt."

  Common Layout Rules:
    WatercolorBase: "Each slide has a light watercolor wash background — blue and red washes blending organically at edges."
    TextArea: "Text is placed on clean white or near-white areas — never directly on saturated wash zones."
    Bleeds: "Watercolor washes bleed beyond imaginary borders — organic, not geometrically contained."
    Brushstrokes: "Decorative brushstroke dividers and underlines — not geometric rules or lines."

Layout Variations:
  - Type: "Watercolor Cover"
    Design: "Large overlapping blue and red watercolor washes fill 60–70% of the slide. Title placed on the white or light area in elegant serif type. Optional: a single fine-line botanical or geometric element as a decorative accent."

  - Type: "Split Wash"
    Design: "Slide divided asymmetrically. Left: deep blue wash (40%). Right: white or light area (60%) with structured text content. Red accent brushstroke serves as the organic divider between zones."

  - Type: "Framed Content"
    Design: "Watercolor painted frame border around the slide edge — blue corners, red details. Inner area is clean white with text and data. Feels like content displayed inside a handmade painted frame."

  - Type: "Data on Paper"
    Design: "Clean off-white background (cold-press paper feel). Charts or tables rendered in blue and red matching the watercolor palette. Data labels in charcoal. Light watercolor circle or blob as visual accent behind key numbers."

  - Type: "Photo with Wash"
    Design: "Black-and-white or desaturated photo. Watercolor washes overlaid at corners and edges (blue top, red bottom or diagonal). Elegant serif caption text below."

  - Type: "Brushstroke Timeline"
    Design: "A broad horizontal brushstroke (blue or red) serves as the timeline axis. Events or milestones appear above and below it as short vertical marks with labels. Organic, painterly, non-mechanical feel."

  - Type: "Quote Splash"
    Design: "Large red watercolor splash as the background element. A meaningful quote in large, elegant serif type centered over the splash. Attribution in small body text below."

Design Rules:
  - "Wet Edges: All watercolor elements must have soft, wet-into-wet bleed edges — no hard-edged shapes painted in flat color."
  - "Translucency: Colors are always translucent, showing texture and layering beneath."
  - "Paper Texture: Background always suggests cold-press watercolor paper texture — off-white, slightly rough."
  - "Two-Color Discipline: Royal blue and crimson red are the only saturated colors. All else is neutral."
  - "No Digital Shine: No gradients, no gloss, no metallic effects — purely painterly and matte."
  - "Generation: Generate a cohesive multi-slide presentation. Maintain consistent watercolor wash style, paper texture, and two-color palette discipline across all slides."
```
