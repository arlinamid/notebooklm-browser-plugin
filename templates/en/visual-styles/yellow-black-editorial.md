# Yellow x Black Editorial

**Source:** Kawai
**Format:** Slide Deck
**Difficulty:** Intermediate

## Prompt

```
# presentation_design_spec_yellow_black_editorial.yaml
# Style: Yellow × Black Editorial
# Concept: "Maximum Contrast, Maximum Impact"

Global Design Settings:
  Tone: "Bold, high-fashion editorial, high-contrast, confident, dynamic"

  Color Palette:
    Base: "#FFD600 (vivid canary yellow — primary canvas)"
    Text:
      Primary: "#000000 (jet black — maximum contrast)"
    Accent:
      - "#FFFFFF (white — secondary text and graphic elements)"
    Rule: "Three-color discipline: Yellow (#FFD600), Black (#000000), White (#FFFFFF) only. No other colors."

  Typography:
    Headings: "Ultra-bold condensed serif or display sans. Black type on yellow zones, white type on black zones."
    Body: "Medium-weight sans serif. Black on yellow, white on black."
    Editorial: "Large English typographic elements as graphic anchors, even in non-English slides."
    Fashion: "Mix of ultra-large and ultra-small text — extreme jump ratio (10:1 minimum)."
    Language: "The language should be what users said in the prompt. English typography used as visual texture."

  Common Layout Rules:
    Contrast: "Every slide achieves extreme visual impact through yellow-black opposition. No soft transitions."
    Photos: "Fashion editorial photography — bold poses, strong directional lighting. Black-and-white or ultra-saturated."
    Stickers: "Handwritten annotations, bold sticker labels, editorial stamps — scattered asymmetrically as accent elements."
    Grid: "Column-based grid with intentional breaks — elements spill across grid lines for dynamism."

Layout Variations:
  - Type: "Editorial Cover"
    Design: "Full yellow background. Massive black serif title occupies 50–70% of slide area. A black-and-white fashion photo overlaps the title partially. White strip at the bottom with subtitle or issue number."

  - Type: "Black Panel Split"
    Design: "50:50 or 60:40 split. Left: black panel with white title and yellow accent rule. Right: yellow panel with fashion photo and black caption. High-contrast hard boundary between panels — no gradient."

  - Type: "Typography Poster"
    Design: "Yellow background. Typographic composition using only text — no images. Different weights and sizes of black type create hierarchy and texture. One key phrase in oversized display type dominates."

  - Type: "Feature Photo"
    Design: "Full-bleed fashion photo (high-contrast, strong pose). Yellow title text overlaid, ultra-bold. Black rectangular caption bar at the bottom. Handwritten annotation or sticker as a decorative accent element."

  - Type: "Data Editorial"
    Design: "Yellow background. A large statistic or percentage in massive black type. Small editorial label above it. A handwritten circle or underline annotation highlights the number. Body text in small, tight columns."

  - Type: "Fashion Grid"
    Design: "4–6 photo tiles arranged in an asymmetric grid. Black gutter lines between tiles. Yellow band across the top or bottom with headline. One tile is black-filled with white text instead of photo."

  - Type: "Bold Statement"
    Design: "Black full-bleed background. One yellow word or short phrase in the largest possible type. A single white thin rule above or below. Nothing else — pure statement slide."

Design Rules:
  - "Three-Color Rule: Yellow (#FFD600), Black (#000000), and White (#FFFFFF) only. Any other color breaks the system."
  - "Extreme Typography: Headlines must be dramatically larger than body text — not gradual difference, but shock contrast."
  - "Fashion Photography: Photos are editorial quality — strong directional lighting, bold fashion styling, never stock imagery."
  - "No Soft Edges: No rounded corners, no soft shadows, no gradients. Everything is sharp and decisive."
  - "Sticker Energy: Handwritten elements and editorial stickers appear scattered — not centered, not symmetrical."
  - "Generation: Generate slides that feel like editorial spreads from a bold fashion magazine. Maintain three-color discipline, extreme typographic contrast, and fashion editorial photography style throughout."
```
